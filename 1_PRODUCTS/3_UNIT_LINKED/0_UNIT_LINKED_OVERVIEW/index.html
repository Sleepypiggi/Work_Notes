    Variable Life¶

    Variable life is similar to universal life where premiums are accumulated inside an investment account. The key difference lies in the nature of the investment account:

    • Insurer’s actually maintains a separate account (from their own) that holds the invested assets on behalf of the policyholder (similar to a custodian)
    • Invested assets are typically in the form of units from one or more mutual funds
    • Account value is based on the total value of all units under the policy

    Unlike universal life, the account value can now fluctuate, passing on the investment risk to the policyholder.

    Note

    It is also known as Unit Linked or investment linked insurance.

    Additionally, mutual funds are interchangeable with unit trusts (negligible difference is in overall ownership of the fund). In Singapore, MAS uses the term Collective Investment Schemes (CIS).

    Unit Account¶

    Policyholders must choose to invest in one or more unit funds. There are three main variations:

    • Self Managed - Insurer forms their own fund, investing in the underlying assets themselves (Equity, Bonds, Cash or other MFs) and issues units for the fund

    • Feeder - Insurer invests directly in an externally managed mutual fund

    • Wrapper - Insurer invests in multiple mutual funds (no other asset types) and issues units of the combined fund

    Note

    Feeder funds are known as such because they feed into a larger centralized master fund for overall management.

    Warning

    Operationally, wrapper funds are considered a self-managed fund because the insurer still manages the combined, wrapped fund themselves.

    A wrapper fund can also contain just a single underlying fund.

    There are several implications:

    • Unit Prices - Feeder funds directly uses the prices of the underlying mutual fund while it is calculated directly for self-managed funds

    • Management Charges - Feeder funds typically directly use the charges of the underlying fund while the insurer can set their own charge for the underlying
